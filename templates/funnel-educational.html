The 5-step outreach that actually gets replies.

Not pitching you anything today. Just sharing what works — based on what we've seen from students who've gotten internship offers through direct outreach:

  1. Target people, not companies. Find team leads and senior ICs. The people with the power to bring someone in aren't always the most senior.
  2. Lead with curiosity, not a CV. "I saw what your team is building at [company] and wanted to connect" opens more doors than "I'm looking for an internship."
  3. Keep the first message short. 2–3 sentences. One clear reason why you're reaching out. One soft ask. Under 100 words.
  4. Follow up once, 5–7 days later. "Just resurfacing this in case it got buried" — this doubles your reply rate.
  5. Volume is a strategy. Students who land internships through outreach send 20–30 messages. Not 5. Not 10. Keep going.

The Outreach Tool handles steps 1–3 for you automatically. The follow-up and the volume are on you.
